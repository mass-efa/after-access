# Safety and Compliance Guide

This toolkit is designed to help people preserve professional memory, not company property.

It is not legal, employment, or compliance advice. Users should follow their employment agreements, separation agreements, company policies, professional obligations, and applicable law.

## Intended Use

Use the prompts to capture:

- Your role in projects and initiatives
- Problems you worked on
- Skills you demonstrated
- Lessons learned
- High-level outcomes you are comfortable remembering
- Interview stories
- External-safe resume bullets
- Categories of evidence that existed

## Do Not Use This Toolkit To Preserve

Do not copy, upload, summarize in sensitive detail, reconstruct, or retain:

- Source code
- Internal specs
- Internal emails
- Confidential roadmap decks
- Architecture diagrams
- Customer confidential data
- Partner confidential data
- Proprietary company documents
- Credentials, access tokens, API keys, or secrets
- Trade secrets
- Non-public financial forecasts
- Personal data about coworkers, candidates, customers, or partners
- Materials covered by NDAs, employment agreements, separation agreements, or company policy restrictions

## Safer Alternatives

Instead of uploading restricted material, describe your work from memory at a high level.

Safer:

> I worked on a pricing platform that supported targeted promotions. My role was product strategy and cross-functional execution. Evidence existed in roadmap docs and performance readouts.

Risky:

> Here is the internal pricing spec, source code, full customer dashboard, and unreleased roadmap.

## Evidence Index, Not Document Dump

The prompts ask for an **Evidence Index** because future resume and interview work usually needs memory anchors, not retained internal files.

An Evidence Index can record:

- Evidence category
- Where evidence existed
- What claim it supports
- Whether the claim is user-provided, inferred, or needs confirmation
- Any external-safe phrasing notes

It should not include the confidential content itself.

## When In Doubt

Leave it out.

Use placeholders like `[TODO: confirm]`, `[User-provided; verify if needed]`, or `[Inference: ...]` instead of turning uncertain or restricted material into a polished claim.

If you are unsure whether a detail is safe to preserve or share, ask a qualified professional before including it.
