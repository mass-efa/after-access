# After Access

Map your work. Keep your story. Leave the confidential stuff behind.

After Access is a prompt toolkit for people who have been laid off, are preparing for a transition, or want to preserve their professional impact before losing access to workplace systems.

This toolkit helps you build a durable, policy-conscious professional archive that can later be turned into resumes, interview stories, LinkedIn content, executive bios, portfolios, consulting profiles, and career narratives.

**Share this toolkit:** https://github.com/mass-efa/after-access

> **Important:** This toolkit is for preserving your professional memory, accomplishments, lessons learned, and career stories. It is **not** for copying, exporting, retaining, or reconstructing confidential company documents, source code, internal specs, internal emails, proprietary roadmaps, customer confidential information, partner confidential materials, credentials, trade secrets, or other restricted information.

> **Not legal advice:** Follow your employment agreements, separation agreement, company policies, professional obligations, and applicable law. If you are unsure whether something is safe to preserve or share, do not include it here. Ask a qualified professional for advice.

---

## Who this is for

Use this if you are:

- Recently laid off or expecting to lose access to workplace systems
- Trying to preserve your professional accomplishments
- Preparing for interviews or recruiter conversations
- Rebuilding your resume from actual impact instead of job descriptions
- Trying to remember metrics, stories, projects, and lessons before access disappears
- Helping colleagues package their work responsibly after a transition

## Recommended path

### If you have time

1. Start with the Modular Archive Builder.
2. Select work inventory, evidence/metrics, case studies, story library, evidence index, positioning, and top accomplishments.
3. Review and correct each phase.
4. Generate a final master archive.
5. Later generate resumes and interview prep from that archive.

### If you have very little time

1. Use Fast Recovery Mode.
2. Capture top projects, metrics, accomplishments, and evidence categories.
3. Then use Interview Story Builder.
4. Return later to build a fuller archive.

## Confidentiality and IP reminder

Do **not** copy or retain:

- Internal specs
- Source code
- Internal emails
- Confidential roadmap decks
- Customer data
- Partner confidential data
- Proprietary company documents
- Architecture diagrams
- Non-public financial forecasts
- Credentials, access tokens, or secrets
- Personal data about coworkers, customers, candidates, or partners

Instead preserve:

- What you worked on
- What your role was
- What you learned
- High-level metrics you are comfortable remembering
- Evidence categories that existed
- Interview-ready stories
- External-safe accomplishment language

See [`SAFETY.md`](SAFETY.md) for the intended boundaries.
